Unfortunalely, HODLing is on hold with the new entrants: Liquidity Pools and Yield Farming.

In Summary: Yield Farming is a way to provide liquid to a certain liquidity pool, and be rewarded for it. It is risky, and this article is not financial advice.

Liquidity Pools and Liquidity Provider

These are “pools” of token locked in a Smart Contract (A program that automatically executes on the network) and are used to provide liquidity to assets. If you’ve ever tried to buy Crypto on an exchange such as Coinbase, you might be aware that these work on the central order book model, the same model that is employed by most big stock exchanges such as NYSE. An order book is maintained by a central organization, where your orders for a certain crypto/stock are updated. When there is shortage of buyers/sellers, we turn to market makers. These real fine league of humans are ready to buy or sell an asset at any point of time.

Without Market Makers, we would have an illiquid market, as in there would be lack of buying and selling activity. It would be hard to sell a stock if t here is no one to buy it. Replicating such a structure on a Blockchain Network such as Ethereum would be slow and expensive (unless you’re using a consensus-less permissioned system).

A Liquidity Provider (LP) is a person who funds a pool with assets to provide liquidity to the pool. They can earn passive income using these platforms.

So what is Yield Farming?

Yield Farming is when you stake or lend Crypto asset to generate high returns. Yield Farming allows LPs to stake their crypto assets in a Liquidity Pool for an incentive that can be either a %age of the transaction fees, interests from giving our loans and the return from increase in value of tokens issued. Yield Farming is usually developed on Ethereum and use ERC-20 (Such as on UniSwap) for liquidity and rewards. The difference between Staking your Ethereum for Networks such as Eth2, investing in EAssets and Yield Farming is that in Yield Farming, you put your money in a decentralized non-custodial protocol. Yield Farmers shift around their money between different pools and protocols quite a bit to optimize for profits.

There are various platforms for Yield Farming such as Compound, Aave, Uniswap and Balancer.

How to start Yield Farming

There are multiple platforms to start off with. Let’s go over a few.

Compound

You can deposit $ETH, get a vault and recieve $DAI which you can store anywwhere.

Curve

There are about 26 Curve Pools currently (You can view them at Curve.fi). Deposite collateral into one of these liquidity pools and stake your token via the Curve Gauge. You;ll in turn get the CRV token. You can lock it via the Curve DAO for increasing your liquidity.

Risks involved in Yield Farming

YF is complicated and risky. On layer 1 Ethereum network, the gas fee can be high and moving around your funds can be expensive. A lot of new projects are also essentially projects that will eventually just be forgotten about. There are problems of impermanent loss, hacking and fraud.
